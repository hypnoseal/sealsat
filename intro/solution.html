Solution

Satellite based infrastructure is, in general, the most cost effective method of delivering connectivity in remote regions. This is due to the ability of satellite providers to shift a large portion of the sunk construction costs to cheaper jurisdictions. Satellites themselves, their associated infrastructure and network operations can be built and developed in more cost effective environments. Unlike land based infrastructure, such as cable or microwave, the construction costs in remote areas for a satellite solution is limited to the rollout of earth stations and metropolitan area networks (MAN). These costs savings allow for: cheaper solutions which provide an ability for the frequent overhaul or enhancement of network hardware; a shift from the costs associated with the construction of cables or towers to improved satellite functionality and capabilities; and, vastly greater coverage.
